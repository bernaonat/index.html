Project Overview

\

\ The "Expert Is In" program is a cornerstone public engagement initiative at the Smithsonian National Museum of Natural History, designed to bridge the gap between cutting-edge research and the curious public. This free program invites leading experts \'96 including scientists, researchers, and artists \'96 directly into the museum's exhibition halls and education centers to share their work on nature-based themes through dynamic, direct engagement with visitors.\

Key Achievements & Program Evolution

\
\

The "Expert Is In" program, originally launched as "Scientist Is In," began as a highly successful mobile station utilizing Discovery Carts in the Ocean Hall. Here, museum scientists and partners facilitated informal, interactive conversations, often incorporating collection objects, visuals, and specialized equipment.

\

Due to its profound success in enriching visitor experience and deepening public understanding of complex exhibition content, the program\'92s flexible framework was since been adapted and expanded to other prominent galleries, including the Hall of Human Origins. This expansion is a testament to the program's effectiveness in fostering direct, meaningful connections between experts and the public, making scientific and artistic knowledge accessible and engaging for all ages.
